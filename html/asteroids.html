More Info

An object's absolute magnitude is the magnitude it would have if it were viewed from exactly 32.6 light-years (or 10 parsecs), with no interference.

Apparent magnitude is how bright an object appears to the human eye.

The lower the number, the brighter the object appears. For example, a magnitude of 1 is brighter than a magnitude of 2. Most stars you see in the night sky have an apparent magnitude around +5. The dimmest stars visible to the naked eye have a magnitude of about +6, With binoculars or beginner telescopes, you can see objects as faint as +10.
